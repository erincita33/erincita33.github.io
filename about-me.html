A Little About Me

I grew up in Chico, California with a pool in my backyard and parents who took us to swimming and diving lessons every summer.  I swam competitively in high school and after that I lifeguarded and taught lessons for 5 summers at various public pools, a preschool, and a waterpark.  in 2008 I started my own swimming lesson business in Utah and I am now in the Bay Area.

 

I had my first experience teaching a private lesson at the Dwight Brinson Swim Center in Durham, California. My supervisor asked me if I wanted to take on private lessons with a 3 year old boy. The very sight of the pool brought on kicking and screaming and tears and no one knew what to do with him. I was excited to see what I could do with this little guy.

 

My excitement didn't last long as I learned quickly that private lessons with him meant having everyone at the pool staring at me for 20 minutes day after day as I tried to figure out what to do with my little screaming banshee. But I was determined and I tried everything I could think of. By the end of the 3 weeks he was loving lessons and the very last day as he walked away holding his dad's hand he looked back at me, gave me a big grin, waved and said, "Bye, teacher!"

 

I have never forgotten that cherished experience and that sweet moment.

 

I went on with college, lived in Bolivia, went on a mission to Portugal, and eventually graduated from BYU with a BA in Latin American Studies. I had quite a few great job experiences in the realms of education and social work. I especially loved my time working with troubled teens in a wilderness and residential programs and with missionaries at the Missionary Training Center. My favorite moments in all those jobs was when I got to work one on one with a teen or a missionary who was struggling. Those are the moments I could see the true miracles happening.


In the summer of 2007 I taught water aerobics for a woman who taught swimming in her backyard pool. I realized how much I loved and missed it and decided to start up my own business. It was one of the best decisions I've ever made!

 

I have loved being able to blend my swimming and swimming lesson experience with my understanding of children and people as unique and individual learners. I have taught in several swim schools and pools and I have been trained in a variety of methods. Through the years I have developed my own unique teaching style that works best for me and I have found to be most effective.

 

I believe in teaching people, not lessons.  There is a difference.  :)  I get to know my students and I find out what motivates them and what their personal learning style is.


I teach all people with a focus on safety.  Floating and buoyancy, as well as healthy respect, but not fear, of the water are the best ways to protect against accidents while in the water.  Some swim instructors neglect these simple skills in favor of teaching competitive strokes.  I have learned that the student's ability to learn competitive strokes is greatly heightened when buoyancy becomes natural.  Not only does it make more sense from a safety perspective, it actually accelerates their technical progress.
